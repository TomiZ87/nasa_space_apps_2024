Transcriptional profiling of livers from mice flown on Rodent Research Reference Mission-1 (RRRM-1)

1. Purpose

The purpose of the study is to investigate the effects of spaceflight on the physiology and biology of mice, specifically observing differences related to age and environmental stressors.

2. Study Design

2.1 Preparation

Mice were housed and maintained under specific conditions at NASA facilities before being grouped for spaceflight or control treatments.

2.2 Sample

Forty female mice were divided by age into two groups with both young (10-12 weeks) and old (32 weeks) mice; half of each age group was sacrificed on the ISS, while the other half returned to Earth for further study.

2.3 Further Investigation

Mice were either sacrificed on the ISS or allowed time to recover on Earth following their return; control groups on the ground provided comparative data against the spaceflight conditions.

3. Dataset

The final dataset includes RNA-sequencing data from the livers of the mice, examining the biological impact of their exposure to spaceflight and ground-based conditions.
