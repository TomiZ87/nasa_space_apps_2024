Transcriptional profiling of livers from mice flown on Rodent Research Reference Mission-1 (RRRM-1)

1. Purpose

The study aimed to evaluate the effects of spaceflight on mice, assessing differences due to age and the impact of environmental conditions during the flight.

2. Study Design

2.1 Preparation

Mice were obtained from Taconic Biosciences, acclimated at NASA's Animal Care Facility, and adapted to conditions simulating spaceflight environments.

2.2 Sample

The study involved 160 female BALB/cAnNTac mice, split into young and old groups, with further divisions for different treatment conditions: on-orbit sacrifice and live return to Earth.

2.3 Further Investigation

Mice were monitored and fed throughout their stay in space and upon return; conditions were matched in ground control groups for comparison.

2.4 Analysis

After spaceflight, some mice were sacrificed for analysis, while others were observed for stresses from environmental changes before collection of liver samples.

3. Dataset

The dataset contains RNA sequencing data from liver samples of mice across different experiment groups, with controls to account for non-spaceflight variables.
