Transcriptional Profiling of Mice Livers in Spaceflight

Study of Gene Expression Changes in Mice Flown Aboard the ISS (RRRM-1 Mission)

Introduction & Objective

Investigating the transcriptional changes in the liver of mice exposed to spaceflight aboard the ISS to understand the biological effects of space conditions.

Experimental Design

Animal Sourcing & Pre-Flight Conditioning

Mice sourced from Taconic Biosciences and prepped at NASA Kennedy Space Center. Weighed, chipped, housed under 12:12 light cycle. Grouped by 5 per cage, fed standard lab chow, and enriched with igloos.

Pre-Flight Simulations & Grouping

Mice adapted to spaceflight conditions with raised mesh flooring and water lixits. Switched to NASA Nutrient-Upgraded Rodent Foodbars (NuRFB). Social acclimation occurred 10 days before launch, with 10 mice per cage.

Grouping & Transport for Spaceflight

Groups identified: Spaceflight, Habitat Ground Control, and Vivarium Ground Control. Spaceflight mice and HGC mice experienced 3-day offsets.

Challenges in Space

Food Contamination & Housing Density Issues

Spaceflight and HGC mice exposed to mold-contaminated food. Mice faced double-density housing due to bad weather, adding stress. Possible sea sickness for Spaceflight mice during return journey.

Sample Collection & Processing

Sample Collection Post-Flight

Mice euthanized and livers dissected into lobes. Livers snap frozen in liquid nitrogen within 10-16 minutes. Samples stored at -80Â°C for transportation.

RNA Extraction & Sequencing

RNA Sequencing & Analysis

RNA extracted using AllPrep DNA/RNA Mini Kit. Homogenization with tissue bead mix, RNA/DNA purified. RNA sequenced on Illumina NovaSeq 6000, paired-end 149 bp reads.

Results & Conclusions

  • Spaceflight-induced gene expression changes in liver tissues.
  • RNA sequencing revealed differential gene expression patterns.
  • Potential long-term impacts on liver function in microgravity environments.
